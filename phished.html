What is this?

If you've been redirected to this page, you've interacted with a Phishing Assessment email. The term "phishing" refers to a cyberattack in which someone tries to impersonate a reputable contact (e.g. Google Accounts, a District Officer, etc) in order to trick the target do something, such as clicking a link to download malware, or transferring money. While this was not an actual phishing email, it was a test by intended to size up the LCUSD staff's ability to detect and report such attacks.

How do I identify and respond to phishing emails?
  1. Check the email of the sender. People within the district should sending emails within the LCUSD domain (lcusd.net or mylcusd.net). In the event that someone claiming to be a student of faculty member uses an outside email domain (e.g. gmail.com, hotmail.com), exercise caution!
  2. If you suspect something, don't click on links or download attached files. In order to check where a link directs, hover over links with your mouse cursor to see where they would lead you to.
  3. Report it. Even if an email looks mostly legitimate but has a few discrepancies such as incorrect email address or uncharacteristic language, forward the email to help@lcusd.net to file a report.
Want to try practicing your identification skills?

You're in luck! Google's got a little phishing quiz that you can use to practice identifying phishing emails. You can find the link here. If you want, try hovering over the link to check its legitimacy. (Don't be excessively paranoid, phishingquiz.withgoogle.com is a legitimate address!)
